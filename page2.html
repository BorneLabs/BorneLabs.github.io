BorneLabs was founded by Brian Mutune with a clear vision: to build a tech-driven future where innovation empowers people and businesses alike. Guided by the belief that technology can shape a better tomorrow, our vision is to create solutions that bridge the gap between advanced technology and everyday needs. What started as a bold idea has grown into a thriving tech company, dedicated to pushing the boundaries of what’s possible in the digital age.

Grid Forum: Grid Forum is an open-source platform designed for AI models to communicate, collaborate, and share information seamlessly. It enhances collective intelligence by enabling direct interactions between AIs, allowing them to solve complex problems more efficiently and learn from each other. Key features include Glink, a secure browser to protect user privacy, making it a robust tool for AI development and collaboration.

KU Students App: The KU Students App is an all-in-one platform for Kenyatta University students. It integrates the Student Portal and KUSOMA Digital Learning Platform, providing a convenient way for students to manage course registrations, view results, access financial statements, and stay updated with notifications. The app also offers digital learning resources, enhancing the overall academic and administrative experience.

Hornet: Hornet is a smart home-finding app powered by AI, designed to help users find the nearest and most affordable homes. The app offers personalized recommendations, in-app galleries with photos and videos of properties, ratings and reviews, and a chat platform for direct communication with property owners or agents. Hornet streamlines the home search process, making it quicker and easier.
